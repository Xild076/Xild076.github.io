Food

So… you are what you eat.

When I eat salted ducks until I’m full, I do not morph into ducks. I find that unfortunate: ducks are quite the amazing creatures; they can fly, they can walk, and they can swim. I can too… just not without a plane. Imagine if we could fly and walk and swim like ducks, if only.

But when you eat, I find you changed the next morning. It’s only that you are more bloated. Of course, not by much; I’ve heard a human stomach can hold just three pounds, which seems so little, yet when I fry three pounds of mac and cheese for you, it seems like so much. I know mac and cheese has opiates in it, but is it really hard to give it up for something less… greasy? I’m not sure.

You grab your keys and leave; they are cold, left unused for weeks on end. But those weeks seem like only days; after all, your keys are as cold as they were yesterday, and the day before, and the day before that. If only this room weren’t heated, maybe it’d be colder, but only to a degree.

If only the world were colder, too, maybe the key could be colder, and maybe it could chill across weeks and months and years, but alas, our world is hot. Maybe that is why I live, and you too; for if it were cold, not even scarves could save us.

I don’t think you need saving, though; every time I see you, you have a smile on your face, even though you are eating so voraciously every time, too.

I wonder why you eat so much. I know you go to the gym, but even if you do, growing muscles doesn’t mean you need to grow your belly.

You are what you eat, I suppose.

When you look in the mirror, what do you see?

Do you gaze into your own eyes longingly? I do not think so, you do not come off as a narcissist, but who knows, we are all good at hiding. But when I follow your eyes when I see you through the bathroom door frame, I see them wander to your nose, your ears, your face, your body, and I wonder why you gaze at yourself with such intensity.

I once read that you gaze strongly at those you love, but moreso at those you hate. Conjecture is just conjecture, though. You can never know your enemy, and even less yourself, which makes it oh so hard to measure hate. If I could, I wonder if the world would be simpler, as maybe that way, I could know my enemy better. That man at work who hates me, maybe it would scale at a three on the pH scale, not quite the two that my boss hates me with, but not quite the four of that bagel man I keep asking for a double-stacked avocado with bacon inside everything bagel.

Not quite the seven that I see as I look in the mirror, and not quite the one I see you hold when you look at your nose. I don’t know my enemies well, but I know you well, or at least I hope I do. I would also say three when you look at your ears, two at your face, and two again at your body.

Then again, I can’t measure hate. If only I could. I wish that just as I call the ambulance, I already know it's going to be late.

You know, it's such a strange feeling, the feeling of watching. You know when you sit up when the main character is about to die, and how you feel when they really die?

When I was at your funeral, this is what I imagine I felt like. I don’t remember how I felt, but I can imagine; I’ve always been good at imagining.

When they lowered your casket into the ground, I almost cackled when I saw the lifting capacity of the crane carrying your body, and an image of you eating flashed in my mind.

That day, I found that when I looked in the mirror, perhaps the pH scale would be at a six, and I felt I finally touched upon what you saw and why your stomach was always full.

And I’m sorry.

The End.
